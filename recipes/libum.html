Libum

What is Libum?

Libum is a sacrificial cheesecake prepared as an offering for the gods. A recipe for the cake was included the agricultural book De Agri Cultura written by the Roman Consul Cato in the 2nd century BCE.

Ingredients:

  • 300 grams of firm cows cheese
  • 150 grams of white wheat flour
  • Half an egg
  • Bay laurel leaves

Instructions:

  1. Cut up the cheese and pound it in a mortar.
  2. Mix the flour in with the cheese, kneading well, and add half an egg.
  3. When the dough has a smooth consistency, flour your work surface then shape the dough into a circular shape.
  4. Coat your cooking dish with bay laurel leaves and lay the libum into it.
  5. Cook in an oven for 20-30 minutes until browned.
  6. Remove the bay laurel leaves and serve hot or warm.
