Laridum and Posca

What is Laridum and Posca?

Laridum (or lardum) and posca were part of the diet of legionaries in the Roman army. Laridum is simmered pork fatback and posca is a mixture of mostly vinegar and water, and it was commonly drank among the lower classes.

Laridum ingredients:

  • Lardo (a type of cured pork fatback)
  • Dry or fresh dill
  • Olive oil
  • Salt

Laridum instructions:

  1. Simmer the lardo for 15 minutes with dill, then discard the water.
  2. Slice the lardo and sprinkle on some olive oil and a pinch of salt before serving.

Posca ingredients:

  • Vinegar
  • Water
  • Dry or fresh pennyroyal or mint

Posca instructions:

  1. Mix seven parts water with one part vinegar.
  2. Add a little bit of pennyroyal or mint.
